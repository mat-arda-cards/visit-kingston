## Rosa Delgado — Los Compadres Cantina

> _"I don't need another app to babysit — I need the whole internet to stop telling people we're open when we're closed, and to send the ferry crowd up my hill hungry."_

**Category:** Full-service restaurant (family Mexican) · **Tech comfort:** Low / tech-averse · **Seasonality:** Year-round · **Size:** Family-run, ~12 staff

I'm Rosa. My husband and I have run Los Compadres Cantina for 18 years, up the hill off the main strip in Kingston. It's a family place — my son cooks, my sister-in-law's on the line, my daughter Marisol runs our Facebook because Lord knows I can't. We're full-service, real plates, and we're open all year, not just when the summer people come. I am proudly old-school. I barely touch my phone, so understand me when I say I'm tired of "solutions." Every app someone talked me into cost money, needed constant fiddling, and then showed the wrong information anyway.

Here's what I actually need, plain: my hours have to be RIGHT everywhere a visitor might look — the app, the town kiosk, Google — and I need to fix them in ten seconds from my phone, or let Marisol or a waiter do it, without calling anybody. Last spring we closed early for my cousin's funeral and the internet still said "open," so a whole ferry-load of walk-ons trudged up the hill to a locked door and left mad reviews. That cannot happen again. And when 200 people walk off a boat all at once, I want a heads-up ten minutes before — not a dining room that goes from empty to chaos with three people on the floor. If this app does those things, stays free to me, and gets out of my way, I'm in. If it's one more thing to maintain, I won't be.

**What success looks like for me:**
- Have my hours be correct and identical everywhere a visitor checks — app, town kiosk, and Google — so nobody ever walks up my hill to a locked door.
- Change today's hours (or mark 'closed early / closed for a private event') in under 10 seconds from my phone, and let my daughter or a trusted employee do it too.
- Stop the constant 'are you open?' and 'how long's the wait?' phone calls that tie up my line during the dinner rush.
- Get a heads-up about 10 minutes before a busy ferry unloads a crowd so I can call in extra hands or fire the line.
- Get discovered by walk-on visitors who don't even know a family Mexican restaurant exists up off the strip.
- Never pay a per-order fee or a monthly bill to be listed or to keep my info right — it has to stay free to me.

**What hurts today:**
- Google, Yelp, and the town's tourist screen show WRONG hours — the day we closed early for a funeral they said 'open,' a ferry crowd came up to a locked door, and I got bad reviews I didn't deserve.
- My phone rings off the hook during the dinner rush with 'are you open?' and 'what's the wait?' — it ties up the one line I need for to-go orders and my host can't keep up.
- A boat lands and ~200 walk-ons hit the strip in a 12-minute burst with zero warning; I go from a quiet room to slammed with a skeleton crew and no time to staff up.
- We're up the hill, not on the waterfront, so walk-ons never find us — they eat at the first place off the dock and never know we're here.
- Every tool I've tried costs money, needs constant fiddling I don't have time for, or is so complicated I give up and it goes stale.
- Most of this stuff is English-only and buried in menus — I want something simple and Spanish-friendly for me and half my staff.

#### US-1 — Bulletproof-correct hours everywhere &nbsp; `P0` · `Cost: Medium` · 🔒 **NON-NEGOTIABLE**

> As a restaurant owner, I want my hours in the app to be the single correct source that also feeds Google and the town kiosk, so that a visitor never sees us as open when we're closed.

- **Why it matters:** Wrong hours are my number-one pain — they cost me a whole ferry crowd, a locked-door scene, and undeserved bad reviews the day of my cousin's funeral. If the app can't guarantee this, none of the rest matters to me.
- **Acceptance criteria:** When I change my hours in the app, the change shows to visitors within a minute and also pushes to my Google Business Profile.; The listing shows a clear, honest 'Open now / Closed' state based on today's actual hours, including a special-close I set.; If my hours haven't been confirmed in a while, the app nudges me instead of silently showing stale hours.; The same corrected hours appear on the in-town kiosk, not a different old copy.
- **How it could work:** There should be ONE place I edit my hours and everything else reads from it — the app card, the kiosk, and Google. I edit; the app syncs to Google for me. I don't want to log into five different websites. A little 'last confirmed' date so I know it's current.
- **Examples / precedents:** Google Business Profile hours + 'special hours' for holidays; OpenTable / Yelp 'Open now' badges driven by the listing; Toast / Square showing one set of hours across all their surfaces

#### US-2 — Ten-second hours edit from my phone &nbsp; `P0` · `Cost: Low` · 🔒 **NON-NEGOTIABLE**

> As a low-tech owner, I want to change today's hours or mark 'closed early' in under ten seconds from my phone, so that I'll actually keep it up to date.

- **Why it matters:** If updating is hard, I won't do it and the info goes stale — that's the whole trap of every tool I've quit. It has to be dead simple or it's worthless to me.
- **Acceptance criteria:** From my phone I can set 'Closed today,' 'Closing early at [time],' or 'Open regular hours' in three taps or fewer.; No new login every time — I stay signed in, big buttons, no tiny menus.; A one-tap 'Back to normal hours' undo so a temporary change never becomes permanent by accident.; It works with big text and is understandable at a glance.
- **How it could work:** Think big buttons, not a form. 'Closed today' / 'Closing early' / 'Special event' as tap tiles. Should work half-asleep before service. Please don't make me type unless I want to add a note like 'closed for a funeral.'
- **Examples / precedents:** Google Business Profile 'Mark as closed for the day' toggle; Square 'temporarily closed' switch; Nextdoor / Facebook one-tap status posts my daughter uses

#### US-3 — Let my daughter and staff update, safely &nbsp; `P1` · `Cost: Low`

> As an owner who doesn't live on her phone, I want to give my daughter and a trusted employee permission to update our hours and events, so that the info stays right even when I'm on the line and not looking at a screen.

- **Why it matters:** I'm not going to be the one poking at a phone during a rush. Marisol already runs our Facebook. The people who'll actually keep this current need their own way in — without me handing over my whole account.
- **Acceptance criteria:** I can invite my daughter and one or two staff to help manage the listing without sharing my password.; Helpers can update hours and events but only I can change bank/contact-level settings.; I can see who changed the hours last and remove someone's access if they leave.; Each helper has their own simple login.
- **How it could work:** Like adding a manager on a Facebook page — send an invite, they get their own login. Give them the 'update hours / post events' powers, keep the sensitive stuff to me. A little 'changed by Marisol at 4:12' note so I trust it.
- **Examples / precedents:** Facebook Page roles (Admin vs Editor) my daughter already uses; Google Business Profile adding a 'Manager'; Square team members with limited permissions

#### US-4 — Ferry-crowd heads-up so I can staff up &nbsp; `P0` · `Cost: Medium`

> As a restaurant near the ferry, I want a heads-up about ten minutes before a busy boat unloads, so that I can call in extra hands or fire the line before the rush hits.

- **Why it matters:** A boat drops 200 walk-ons in a 12-minute burst with no warning and I'm slammed with a skeleton crew. Ten minutes' notice is the difference between a good night and a disaster and bad reviews.
- **Acceptance criteria:** I get a simple alert (text or app notification) when an inbound boat is predicted busy, timed so I have ~10 minutes before walk-ons reach the strip.; The alert tells me roughly how busy — 'big crowd' vs 'light' — not just that a boat is coming.; I can turn alerts off on days I don't want them or set which hours I care about.; It's based on the real inbound boat, not a guess that cries wolf.
- **How it could work:** The app already predicts ferry busyness — just point that at me the operator. A plain text like 'Busy boat landing ~6:10, expect walk-ons ~6:20.' No jargon. Let me pick a phone number so I don't have to have the app open. If the prediction's shaky, say so rather than false-alarm me into ignoring it.
- **Examples / precedents:** WSDOT ferry alerts / the app's own busyness forecast; Airport-style 'flight landing' pushes; A text-message alert like a reservation SMS, but for the incoming crowd

#### US-5 — Call-ahead / waitlist so my phone stops ringing &nbsp; `P1` · `Cost: Medium`

> As an owner whose one phone line gets tied up, I want visitors to join a waitlist or see the current wait in the app, so that people stop calling during the dinner rush to ask if we're open and how long the wait is.

- **Why it matters:** The 'are you open / how long's the wait' calls tie up the line I need for to-go orders and my host can't keep up. Let the app answer it.
- **Acceptance criteria:** A visitor can see a rough current wait and add their name to a waitlist without phoning us.; My host can update the wait or clear the list from the same simple phone screen.; We can turn the waitlist off instantly when we're slow or short-staffed.; A guest gets a text when their table's close, so nobody crowds the door.
- **How it could work:** I don't need full reservations — we're a walk-in family place. Just 'wait is about 25 min, put my name down.' Host taps a number to set the wait. Must be optional and killable on a bad night so it never promises seats we don't have.
- **Examples / precedents:** Yelp Waitlist; Toast / Square waitlist with SMS 'your table is ready'; OpenTable waitlist (without full reservations)

#### US-6 — Get found by walk-ons who don't know we exist &nbsp; `P1` · `Cost: Low`

> As a restaurant up the hill off the strip, I want to show up when walk-on visitors browse 'places to eat near the ferry,' so that people who'd never wander up find us.

- **Why it matters:** We're not on the waterfront, so walk-ons eat at the first place off the dock and never learn there's a real family Mexican place five minutes up. Being findable is how I get new customers off every boat.
- **Acceptance criteria:** We appear in the app's 'Eat & Drink' and map results for people arriving by ferry, with a clear 'X min walk from the dock.'; Our listing shows a photo, cuisine, price, and the honest open/closed state.; A visitor can tap for walking directions from the dock right to our door.; We're not buried below only-waterfront places — up-the-hill spots are visible too.
- **How it could work:** Just make sure the map and the eat list send people up the hill, not only along the water. A 'family Mexican, ~6 min walk up' tag. Directions that actually route up the hill. A nice photo of our patio would do more than any words.
- **Examples / precedents:** Google Maps 'restaurants near me' with walk time; Yelp category + distance filtering; Airbnb host guidebook 'local favorites up the hill' recommendations

#### US-7 — Simple, Spanish-friendly, and no upkeep treadmill &nbsp; `P1` · `Cost: Low`

> As a tech-averse owner, I want the whole owner side to be simple and available in Spanish, so that I and my Spanish-speaking staff can use it without a manual and without it becoming a chore I abandon.

- **Why it matters:** Every tool I've quit died because it was complicated, English-only, and needed constant fiddling. Half my kitchen speaks Spanish first. If it's not simple and bilingual, it goes stale within a month and we're back to wrong hours.
- **Acceptance criteria:** The owner screens can switch to Spanish.; The main things I do — hours, waitlist, events — are reachable in one or two taps, no digging.; There's a 'nothing to do today' calm state so I'm not nagged when everything's already right.; A real person at the Chamber I can call if I'm stuck, not just a help article.
- **How it could work:** Keep MY side to a handful of big buttons. Spanish toggle. And please — no weekly 'action items' guilt-tripping me. If my info's current, leave me alone. A phone number for a Chamber human beats any FAQ.
- **Examples / precedents:** Square's Spanish-language POS interface; Facebook / WhatsApp Business language settings my daughter uses; Google Business Profile's plain 'your info looks up to date' state

#### US-8 — See if being listed actually brings people in &nbsp; `P2` · `Cost: Low`

> As an owner deciding whether this app is worth my time, I want a dead-simple monthly count of how many visitors viewed us, tapped for directions, or joined our waitlist, so that I can see it's actually sending customers up the hill.

- **Why it matters:** I've been burned by tools that promised traffic and delivered a bill. Show me plain numbers — views, directions, waitlist joins — and I'll believe it. No dashboards, just 'this many people found you.'
- **Acceptance criteria:** Once a month I can see how many people viewed my listing, got directions, and joined the waitlist.; The numbers are shown in plain language, not charts I have to decode.; No personal data on individual visitors — I don't want that and don't want the liability.; I can get it as a simple text or email, not only by logging in.
- **How it could work:** Like the little 'your post reached 340 people' summary Facebook sends. One text a month: 'In June, 512 people saw Los Compadres, 88 got directions, 40 joined the waitlist.' That's it. Keep it privacy-first — no tracking my customers around.
- **Examples / precedents:** Facebook Page monthly 'insights' summary; Google Business Profile 'X people called / asked for directions'; Square's plain monthly sales/visits recap

**Rosa Delgado's non-negotiables:**
- 🔒 It must NEVER display wrong hours — one editable source of truth that also corrects Google and the town kiosk, or I'm out.
- 🔒 Updating my hours must be trivially easy — under ten seconds from my phone, big buttons, or I won't keep it current and it'll go stale.
- 🔒 It must be free to me — no per-order fees, no monthly bill to be listed or to keep my info right.
- 🔒 It must be simple and Spanish-friendly, and it must not become one more thing I have to constantly maintain.

## Danny Cho — Friends & Neighbors Brewing

> _"If I can post an event ONCE and it hits the town calendar, Instagram, Facebook, and Google without me copy-pasting it four times, I'm in — otherwise it's just one more dashboard I'll stop logging into by August."_

**Category:** Brewery / taproom · **Tech comfort:** High / early adopter · **Seasonality:** Year-round, patio-seasonal · **Size:** ~8 staff + rotating food trucks

I'm Danny, co-owner of Friends & Neighbors Brewing right off the main strip in Kingston. We're open year-round, but our patio and our whole vibe swing hard with the seasons — dead-quiet Tuesdays in February, then a July 4th where the ferry dumps a few hundred walk-ons on us in twelve-minute waves. We've got no kitchen, so our entire food strategy is rotating food trucks and sending people down the block to Grub Hut or Los Tres when the truck's not here. I run all my own marketing: I shoot the reels, I make the event graphics, I answer the DMs. I'm the tech-comfortable one in this coalition — I'll happily wire up an API, I use Untappd and Square, and I'll pay a few bucks a month if the ROI is obvious.

Here's my problem: I am drowning in duplicate posting. Every trivia night, tap takeover, and live-music Friday gets typed FOUR separate times — Instagram, Facebook, Google Business Profile, and the Chamber's town calendar — and half the time the town one is stale because I forgot. I have zero real data on whether any of it works: did the 300 people who walked past us off the 5:15 boat actually come in, or did they all flow to the coffee shop? What I need from this app is brutally simple to say and apparently hard to get: post an event once and have it syndicate everywhere, a live tap list and 'who's the food truck today' people can check before they walk over, analytics I actually own, and a real channel to cross-promote with the other Kingston businesses instead of us all shouting into our own little silos. If it becomes just one more thing I have to babysit, I'm out.

**What success looks like for me:**
- Enter an event (live music, trivia, tap takeover) ONE time and have it automatically appear on the town calendar, my Google Business Profile, and ideally push to Instagram/Facebook — so I stop retyping the same thing four times
- See real foot-traffic and engagement data I control: how many people viewed our listing, tapped 'directions,' or checked the tap list after a boat landed — and whether ferry-arrival bursts actually convert into walk-ins
- Publish a live tap list and today's food-truck lineup that visitors can check from the ferry or the strip before deciding where to go
- Set up B2B cross-promotion with other Kingston spots — when the food truck's not here, my listing points to Grub Hut/Los Tres, and their listings can point back to us for a beer after
- Turn ferry walk-on bursts into measurable taproom traffic during peak season, and fill the dead winter weeknights with events that actually get discovered
- Never have to babysit a second silo — this either syncs with the tools I already use or it's dead to me by August

**What hurts today:**
- I post every single event FOUR times — Instagram, Facebook, Google Business Profile, and the Chamber town calendar — and the town one is constantly out of date because it's the one I forget
- I have no idea which of my promotions actually drives foot traffic; I'm flying blind on ROI for every reel and boosted post
- Food-truck scheduling is chaos — trucks cancel or swap last-minute, and visitors show up expecting food that isn't here, then blame us
- When we have no food truck, I want to cleanly send people to Grub Hut or Los Tres, but there's no easy channel to cross-promote with other Kingston businesses
- Ferry riders walk right past us in huge waves and I can't tell whether they even know we exist, let alone whether they came in
- Every 'community platform' I've tried becomes a dead dashboard I stop logging into — I need this to plug into what I already run, not replace it

#### FNB-01 — Post-once, publish-everywhere events &nbsp; `P0` · `Cost: High` · 🔒 **NON-NEGOTIABLE**

> As a taproom owner, I want to enter an event once in the business portal and have it syndicate to the town calendar, my Google Business Profile, and my social channels, so that I stop typing the same trivia night into four places.

- **Why it matters:** This is the single biggest time-sink in my week and the reason the town calendar is always stale. Fix this and you've earned my login.
- **Acceptance criteria:** Creating one event in the portal makes it appear on the public town/app calendar within minutes; The same event pushes to my connected Google Business Profile as an event/post; At minimum, the app generates a ready-to-share image + caption (or a shareable link) for Instagram/Facebook, and ideally auto-posts via a connected account; Editing or canceling the event once updates every destination, and the app tells me where it published
- **How it could work:** Portal event form (title, date/time, recurring rule, image, description, ticket link) writes to the app calendar and an outbound iCal/JSON feed. Google Business Profile sync via their API for the event post. For Meta, either a proper Graph API connection or, as a fallback, a one-tap 'share to Instagram/Facebook' with pre-filled caption + auto-generated graphic. Recurring events (weekly trivia) must be one entry, not 52.
- **Examples / precedents:** The Events Calendar plugin on the Chamber's WordPress site already exposes a public REST feed — extend that pattern; Eventbrite / Google Business Profile event posts; Later / Buffer for the social auto-post fallback, Canva-style auto-generated event graphic

#### FNB-02 — Analytics I actually own &nbsp; `P0` · `Cost: Medium` · 🔒 **NON-NEGOTIABLE**

> As an operator, I want a simple dashboard showing views, direction taps, tap-list checks, and event RSVPs for my listing — broken down against ferry-arrival times — so that I finally know which promotions and which boats drive foot traffic.

- **Why it matters:** I run all my own marketing and I'm flying blind. Give me numbers I control and I'll actually spend smarter.
- **Acceptance criteria:** I can see listing views, 'directions' taps, tap-list opens, and event clicks over a chosen date range; Engagement can be overlaid against WSF boat-arrival times so I can see the post-ferry spikes; Data is exportable (CSV) and stays mine — privacy-first, no creepy individual tracking; I can compare an event day vs a normal day at a glance
- **How it could work:** Reuse the app's existing privacy-first, coarse-geo analytics — I don't need names, I need aggregate patterns. A per-business tab in my portal with a few charts. The killer feature is joining my engagement timeline to the live ferry-arrival data the app already has, so 'ferry rider who saw us -> walked in' becomes visible, even approximately.
- **Examples / precedents:** Google Business Profile Insights; Square Dashboard analytics; Untappd for Business venue stats

#### FNB-03 — Live tap list &nbsp; `P1` · `Cost: Low`

- **How it could work:** Dead-simple mobile editor in the portal: beer name, style, ABV, price, on/off toggle. Bonus: optional import from an Untappd venue menu so tech-savvy taprooms don't double-enter. Must be phone-first — I'm updating this from behind the bar.
- **Examples / precedents:** Untappd for Business digital menus; Square item availability toggles; Toast online menu 86'ing

#### FNB-04 — Food-truck 'who's here today' slot &nbsp; `P1` · `Cost: Low`

> _"Love the app, but it's a brochure for day-trippers who leave at 4pm; I'm trying to sell people who'd actually LIVE here, and right now the app gives them nothing."_

**Category:** Real estate / professional services (relocation broker) · **Tech comfort:** Moderate-high · **Seasonality:** Year-round · **Size:** Broker plus a small team (two agents, one part-time transaction coordinator)

I'm Linda Cho-Ramirez, the broker-owner of North Sound Realty. I've been selling homes in north Kitsap for eleven years, and honestly, I don't sell houses so much as I sell Kingston. Nine out of ten of my buyers are relocators, usually a Seattle or Bellevue household that's tired of the price-per-square-foot math and has finally clued into the ferry. My whole job is answering the same twenty questions over and over: What are the schools like? How brutal is the commute, really? Is there a dentist, an urgent care, a vet? Can my kid do soccer here? Is there anything to DO in January? I'm the person who convinces someone that a 40-minute boat ride is a lifestyle, not a penalty. I do this with a binder of printouts, my own Google Doc of links, and a lot of driving-around.

I'm comfortable with technology: I run my listings on the NWMLS, I use DocuSign and a CRM, I build my own Facebook and Instagram ads, and I keep a Notion "Kingston relocation guide" I share with clients. So I'm not scared of this app, I'm frustrated by it. It's beautiful, and it's aimed entirely at the person who's here for four hours between ferries. What I need is a "Living Here" side of the house: accurate, current facts on schools, services, utilities, medical, worship, community groups, and the honest commute reality, plus a place to put my open houses so the town's own calendar actually reflects that homes are for sale. If the Chamber's mission is a thriving Kingston, then keeping the people who visit is at least as important as the people who leave.

**What success looks like for me:**
- A dedicated 'Living Here' / resident mode that answers the top relocation questions (schools, commute, utilities, medical, community) accurately, so I can send prospects one link instead of my 12-page Notion doc
- An honest, data-backed commute-to-Seattle picture (ferry schedule, walk-on vs drive-on reality, parking, sailing-cancellation history) that sets expectations before someone falls in love and then panics
- My open houses and new listings visible on the town events calendar and map, so relocators browsing the app discover that Kingston homes are actually available
- Neighborhood / community guides (what it's like to live in each part of town) that I can point buyers to and that make Kingston feel like a community, not just a ferry stop
- A cross-referral loop where a visitor who loved their day trip can flip into 'thinking about moving here?' and land on resident content
- Proof to my clients and to the Chamber that this app serves LOCALS, so residents actually open it in the off-season when tourism dies but my business doesn't

**What hurts today:**
- The entire app assumes you're leaving on the 4:40 boat; there's nothing for the person deciding whether to build a life here, which is 90% of my business
- I answer the exact same relocation questions by hand every week (schools, commute, medical, cost, community) with a homemade Notion doc that's always going stale
- My open houses and listings appear NOWHERE the public looks; they're buried in the NWMLS and Zillow, not on any Kingston-owned calendar or map
- Prospects get an unrealistic or scary picture of the ferry commute from random forum posts, and I spend the first showing just correcting ferry myths
- The town has no single trustworthy source for 'what's it like to LIVE here,' so newcomers form impressions from Nextdoor drama and outdated blog posts
- In winter, when tourism dies, there's no reason for residents or would-be residents to open the app at all, so it goes cold exactly when I'm still working
