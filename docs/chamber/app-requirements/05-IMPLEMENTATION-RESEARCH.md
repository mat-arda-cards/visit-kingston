## Bulletproof hours & 'open now' single source of truth `P0` · `Cost: Low` · 🔒 · _enhances existing_

One owner-editable source of truth for hours, holiday closures, and today's status that also feeds Google Business Profile and the in-app kiosk, so a visitor never sees 'open' at a locked door. The app must show an 'open now, X-min walk from the ferry' state and hide or flag any datum it can't confirm is current.

**Requested by:** Rosa Delgado, Carol Whitfield, Hank Boyd, Sam Okafor, Meg Sorensen, Dr. Aisha Bello, Danny Cho

### Approach

I'm Rosa — I run a little counter-service spot two minutes off the walk-off ramp, and here's the thing that keeps me up: a boat lands, forty people walk up the hill, and if this app tells even five of them I'm "open" when my door's locked, they're mad at ME, not the app. So I don't want anything fancy. I want the app to only ever say "open" when it's actually true, and when it can't be sure, I'd rather it say nothing than guess. Good news — your own docs tell me the bones are already here. You've got structured `weeklyHours` per business, the `getOpenStatus()` math that computes open/closed in Pacific time, the live green "Open now" badge, the "verified [date]" stamp, and even the walk-minutes-from-the-ferry number on my listing. **Build on that. Do not rebuild it.** This epic is three additions to something that already mostly works.

**Addition one — the one-tap "not today" toggle.** My regular hours live in the portal already. What I need is a big dumb button on my phone: "Closed today," "Closing early (pick a time)," and for the gas dock and a couple others "Out of fuel." I tap it, and by the time the next boat lands the app has flipped me to closed — and it clears itself at midnight so I never have to remember to switch it back on, because I *will* forget. You literally already built this exact pattern for the ferry boarding-pass override: a single record stamped with the Pacific day, honored only while that day is still today, lapsing at the next midnight with no timers and no daylight-savings headaches. Copy that store almost verbatim into a `hours-override` store keyed to my listing, have `getOpenStatus()` check it first, and force the badge to "Closed — [reason] today" when it's set. That's the heart of the whole ask and it's a known, proven shape in your code.

**Addition two — holidays I can set once, in advance.** Separate from the same-day panic button, let me punch in "closed July 4, closed Thanksgiving" ahead of time as date-keyed exceptions on my hours record. On that calendar day the open-now math treats me as closed regardless of my weekly pattern. This is the one piece of genuinely new data (there's no holiday/closure field on the listing type today), but it's a small, well-bounded add.

**Addition three — never show a stale guess.** Right now the app shows my "verified [date]" as a friendly stamp, but nobody enforces it. I want a hard rule: if my structured hours are older than some window (say 60–90 days, your call), the app hides the green "Open now" badge and shows a plain "Hours unverified — call ahead · last checked [date]" instead of confidently rendering a guess. That's the acceptance criterion about hiding what you can't confirm, and it's a filter on the badge and the walk-time line, not a schema change. And the walk-minutes-from-ferry number only renders next to a badge the system actually trusts. **On the Google side:** your syndication doc is honest that the GBP write path is *planned but not wired* — there's zero adapter code today. Finishing it is real work gated on the Chamber's own API-access approval and the "Chamber-as-Manager" auth model, so treat GBP as a fast-follow behind a feature flag: ship the in-app single-source-of-truth first (that's what stops the locked-door problem), then push the same hours record out to GBP via a one-way write when access lands. The in-app kiosk already reads the same overlay store, so it's covered for free the moment the store is right.

**Build vs buy:** BUILD the in-app single source of truth; INTEGRATE (one-way write) to Google as a flagged fast-follow; do NOT deep-link out for the core status. The whole point of this P0 is that WE own the truth so a visitor never sees 'open' at a locked door — you cannot outsource that to a third party and still guarantee it. And you're not starting cold: structured hours, the open-now math, the verified-date stamp, the walk-time field, and (critically) the boarding-pass-override auto-expiry pattern all already ship in the repo, so the same-day toggle and holiday exceptions are extensions, not a new system. Google Business Profile is a genuine write integration but it's outbound-only and gated on Chamber API approval, so wire it behind a feature flag after the in-app path is solid — it makes the town's Google results match, but it is not what fixes the locked-door problem. The in-app kiosk needs nothing extra: it already reads the same overlay store, so a correct store is a correct kiosk.

**Rough effort:** Core in-app work (same-day auto-expiring override store + one-tap owner panel + holiday-date exceptions + staleness-hides-the-badge filter, all reusing existing stores/math): ~3-5 dev-days. Finishing the live GBP write path (adapter, Chamber-as-Manager OAuth, pending-edit read-back loop, feature flag, one-way sync on save): ~4-7 dev-days once the Chamber's API access is approved — call the GBP piece a fast-follow, not a blocker. Chamber-side (recruiting owners to add the Chamber as a GBP Manager) is ongoing, low-tech outreach. · **Rough cost:** One-time: developer time only (roughly a dev-week for the in-app core, another for GBP). Recurring: ~$0 net-new — it rides the existing ~$7/mo Render (or Vercel) hosting and ISR; GBP APIs and the DIY override store are free. No per-transaction or SaaS fees. Only cost inflation risk is if you build the optional POS pull, which pulls in a member's own Toast/Square subscription (not the Chamber's cost).

### Real-world examples

- **Google Business Profile — 'Special hours' + real-time 'temporarily closed / hours might differ'** — The gold standard visitors already expect: owner sets holiday/special hours in advance and a same-day status; Google surfaces 'Hours might differ' and hides confident open/closed when it's unsure rather than guessing. This is exactly the behavior we're mirroring in-app and syndicating to. (https://support.google.com/business/answer/6303076)
- **Yelp — 'Special Hours' and 'Temporarily Closed' owner controls** — Lets an owner mark holiday hours and a temporary closure that overrides the weekly pattern, and shows a plain closed/updated state instead of a stale 'open'. Good precedent for a one-tap override that supersedes regular hours. (Note: Yelp has no write API for us — this is a UX precedent only, per our syndication doc.) (https://biz.yelp.com)
- **OpenStreetMap opening_hours spec + evaluator** — The reference open-source model for structured hours including 'PH off' (public-holiday closed) and time-limited exceptions, with a well-tested evaluator. A sanity-check for our holiday/exception data model and edge cases like split shifts and past-midnight closes we already handle. (https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:opening_hours)
- **go.visitbainbridgeisland.org (the neighboring competitor tourism app)** — A live Kingston-adjacent tourism PWA with per-listing hours and open/closed state; useful as the direct comparable a Chamber board member will pull up. Validates that an 'open now' state is table-stakes for a WA ferry-town app and that our verified-only posture is a differentiator. (https://go.visitbainbridgeisland.org)

**LTAC funding:** Squarely LTAC lodging-tax fundable: this is tourism INFORMATION/PROMOTION — an accurate open-now directory and its syndication to Google are exactly the marketing-platform use RCW 67.28 allows, benefiting overnight and 50+-mile visitors. It stays clean because it channels no money to individual for-profits and takes no per-transaction cut; the moment any 'order/pay' feature attaches a commission that would be the risky part, but pure hours/status/syndication is not it.

**Risks:** Owner adoption and freshness — the whole model dies if members don't tap the button or keep hours current. The auto-expiring same-day toggle (self-clears at midnight) and the staleness rule (hide the badge past ~60-90 days rather than show a guess) are the mitigations; make the toggle a one-tap, phone-first, offline-tolerant control or tiny seasonal operators won't use it.; GBP write path is unbuilt and externally gated — no adapter code exists yet, and it needs the Chamber's own verified 60+-day GBP plus an approved Basic API Access application, a ~7-day new-Manager cooldown, a 10-edits/min cap, and a pending-moderation read-back loop. Don't let 'finish GBP' block shipping the in-app fix; flag it as a fast-follow.; Edge cases in the open-now math when overrides stack — a same-day 'closed early' override plus a split shift plus a past-midnight close plus a holiday exception must compose correctly and always fail safe to 'closed/unverified', never to a false 'open'. Reuse and unit-test the existing getOpenStatus() rather than forking it.; Two-way drift if GBP or a POS is ever allowed to write back — keep the portal record strictly canonical and GBP strictly outbound (one-way). A bidirectional sync (or trusting a POS as truth) reintroduces exactly the 'which source is right' drift this epic exists to kill.

## Ten-second, delegated, low-tech self-service portal `P0` · `Cost: Low` · 🔒 · _enhances existing_

A mobile-first owner portal simple enough that a tech-averse or 70-year-old user edits hours, status, menu, or listing in under two minutes with big buttons and no manual — and can delegate that access to a daughter, trusted employee, or rotating volunteer without IT setup. Fits service businesses (broker, clinic) not just restaurants.

**Requested by:** Rosa Delgado, Carol Whitfield, Hank Boyd, Sam Okafor, Meg Sorensen, Tom Iverson, Nadia Fischer, Linda Cho-Ramirez, Dr. Aisha Bello, Marcus Reed

### Approach

I'm Rosa — I run a little storefront on the strip, and I'm the one asking for this loudest, but honestly the person who needs it most isn't me: it's my mom, who covers the register on the days I drive to Poulsbo, and it's Carol at 71 who still writes her hours on an index card taped to the door. So build this as an **enhancement of the portal that already exists**, not a new app. You already have invite-based accounts, scrypt passwords, HMAC session cookies, and per-listing editing living in `src/lib/auth.ts` over the same JSON/DB overlay seam as everything else. The three things missing are: (1) owners can't hand out access themselves — only the Chamber can mint invites today (the invites route is `requireAdmin`-gated); (2) there's one flat `role` + `linkedIds[]` per person, so I can't give my daughter "edit hours only" without making her a full manager; and (3) every profile shape assumes a restaurant or storefront, so Sam the broker and Dr. Bello's clinic don't fit. Fix those three and you've delivered the whole epic cheaply, because the plumbing is done.

For **delegation**, add an owner-initiated invite: on my listing's edit page, a big "Add a helper" button that lets me create a scoped sub-account link tied to just my listing(s), with a role I pick (Full, or "Hours & status only"). Extend the existing `InviteCode`/`User` records with an optional `scope` (which fields the account may touch) and let a `business` owner — not just admin — mint invites for ids they already manage. **Don't make me think about passwords for other people.** Send the helper a magic link by email (you already plan to use Resend, free tier covers 3,000/mo — this is a handful of emails a season) so my mom taps a link and she's in; keep the existing scrypt password path as the fallback for anyone who wants one. Magic-link email beats SMS here: no per-message cost, works on any device, and NIST has deprecated SMS OTP anyway. Revoke has to be one tap and instant — a "Remove" button next to each helper that deletes the sub-account and kills the session. That single flow satisfies "grant and revoke in minutes without calling the Chamber," including the Farmers Market handing the weekly lineup to a rotating volunteer.

For the **service-business shape**, don't bolt "area served" onto the Restaurant type — add a lightweight `profileTemplate` discriminator (`storefront | food | service | market`) that swaps *which fields the form shows*. A service profile shows contact, area served ("Kingston + north Kitsap"), and a status line ("booking through August") instead of cuisine/price-level/menu. Same store, same overlay, same syndication feeds — just a different field set and a different card renderer. That keeps Marcus the broker and the clinic first-class without forking the data model.

Finally, the **ruthless mobile pass** is the actual product here, and it's mostly design, not code. Every core edit — hours, "closed today," status line, a menu item, the description — has to be reachable in one or two taps from a phone home screen, thumb-sized targets (44px+), no nested menus, no jargon, and it has to work on the ferry with two bars. Since it's already a PWA with offline plumbing on the roadmap, make edits queue-and-sync so a save on a dead signal doesn't vanish. The one screen that earns its keep: a giant **"Closed today / Open"** toggle and an **hours** editor a 70-year-old finishes without a manual. If Carol can flip "closed today" from the parking lot when the pipes freeze, you've won.

**Build vs buy:** Build in-app — this is the one epic you should absolutely not outsource. The delegation/scoping logic is a direct extension of `src/lib/auth.ts` (add optional scope + profileTemplate to the InviteCode/User records, relax the invite route so a business owner mints invites for ids they already hold, add a magic-link redeem path). Buying an auth SaaS (Auth0, Clerk, WorkOS) would add a recurring bill, a privacy dependency, and login friction that fights the whole 'dead-simple for a 70-year-old' goal — the opposite of what these ten owners asked for. The only thing worth pulling in is Resend, which is already your planned mailer, for the magic link. Deep-linking out to Google Business Profile for delegation is a fine complement (you syndicate there anyway) but can't be the answer, because the whole point is the owner edits their Visit Kingston listing without leaving the app or learning a second tool.

**Rough effort:** 2-3 dev-weeks total: ~4-6 dev-days for owner-minted scoped invites + magic-link redeem + revoke; ~2-3 dev-days for the profileTemplate discriminator and the service-business form/card; ~4-6 dev-days for the ruthless mobile-simplicity pass (big-button edit screens, 'closed today' toggle, offline queue-and-sync) which is mostly design/QA. The mobile pass is the part that's easy to under-scope. · **Rough cost:** One-time: the dev effort above (internal). Recurring: ~$0 — magic-link email fits Resend's free tier at Kingston's volume (a few dozen invites a season), and it rides the existing ~$7/mo Render hosting with no new service. Effectively no marginal run cost.

## Post-once, publish-everywhere events (incl. recurring) `P0` · `Cost: Medium` · 🔒 · _enhances existing_

Enter an event one time and have it become the authoritative record that syndicates to the town calendar, Google Business Profile, and social channels, with recurring events (Trivia Tuesdays) as a single repeating entry. Kills the 'I type trivia night into four places' problem and stale, contradictory listings.

**Requested by:** Danny Cho, Nadia Fischer, Linda Cho-Ramirez, Marcus Reed, Priya Nair

### Approach

I'm Danny — I run trivia and live-music nights, and right now I type "Trivia Tuesdays, 7pm" into our Facebook page, our Google listing, the Chamber calendar, and then it's already wrong on one of them by the time summer hits. So hear me plainly: **do not build a new system. Finish the one you already have.** The app already has the event calendar, the per-listing JSON + iCal feeds (`/api/feeds/events`), the paste-in embed widget, and the honest `/portal/syndicate` hub with the GBP and Meta plans already researched. The gap the acceptance criteria describe — one repeating entry instead of 52 copies, and a real push to Google/social — is three specific enhancements bolted onto that, not a rebuild.

**First, recurrence, because that's the part that makes me want to throw my phone.** Today the seed data literally stores every Sunday market as its own row (`public-market-2026-07-05`, `-07-12`, `-07-19`…). That's the exact thing I refuse to do by hand for a year. Add an optional `recurrence` field to the `EventItem` model that stores a standard RRULE string (e.g. `FREQ=WEEKLY;BYDAY=TU;UNTIL=20260919`), keep the single record as the authoritative entry, and **expand occurrences at read time** using the battle-tested `rrule.js` library — in `getEvents()`, in the events page grouping, and in the deconfliction check (`eventsSharingDate`) so it sees the expanded dates. In the portal form I want three dumb controls: "Repeats: Never / Weekly / Monthly," a weekday picker, and an "until" date. When I cancel one week (holiday) I add an EXDATE; when I move the whole thing I edit one place. The outbound iCal emitter already hand-folds lines correctly — it just needs to emit the `RRULE` (and `EXDATE`) line per VEVENT instead of one VEVENT per week, which is what every calendar app expects anyway.

**Second, the actual "publish everywhere" push, done Chamber-side, not per-owner.** SYNDICATION.md already nailed the model: the Chamber applies once for Google Business Profile API access and each of us just adds the Chamber's Google account as a **Manager** of our profile — one Chamber login covers all of us, no OAuth ceremony for me. Build the GBP write adapter behind a feature flag: hours via Business Information API v1, and events/posts via the v4 `localPosts` endpoint, which now supports **recurring posts natively** (weekly/monthly `RecurrenceInfo`) — so my Trivia Tuesday maps to one recurring GBP post, not a manual weekly grind. The non-negotiable engineering detail is the **pending-edit read-back loop** (`hasPendingEdits`): if Google silently queues my edit in moderation and nobody checks, the app is quietly lying about "post once, everywhere," and that's worse than doing nothing. Social goes exactly as the doc says — a Meta **pilot with ≤50 tester businesses** (Facebook feed posts, since there's no Facebook Events API, plus Instagram), measure whether owners like me actually want auto-posting before anyone signs up for the multi-week Advanced Access review. Until an adapter is live for a given channel, the honest `/portal/syndicate` copy-and-deep-link path stays the answer — never flip that "no auto-sync yet" callout before the code ships.

**Third, let the rest of us in.** Right now events post through business or nonprofit portals; the realtor doing an open house and the pharmacy running a flu-clinic day (Marcus, Priya) have nowhere to go. Add a lightweight "community poster" role/flow that writes the same `EventItem` to the same calendar and map, with light admin moderation so it doesn't turn into spam. Same authoritative record, same feeds, same map pin — just more of us allowed to feed it.

**Build vs buy:** BUILD the recurrence model in-app; INTEGRATE (Chamber-side) for the outbound push; DEEP-LINK OUT as the honest fallback until each adapter is live. Recurrence and self-service community posting belong in the app because they're small enhancements to code that already exists (the EventItem model, the feeds, the portal form, the map) and because the calendar being the authoritative single source is the whole point — you can't outsource that. The Google/social push should be integration against Google Business Profile and Meta using the Chamber-as-Manager / pilot-tester model already researched in SYNDICATION.md, not a bespoke build and not a paid aggregator by default (an aggregator re-introduces the drift and vendor cost we're trying to kill). Everywhere an adapter isn't live yet, keep the existing copy-paste + deep-link path and its honest 'no auto-sync yet' labeling — deep-linking out is the correct, non-deceptive default, and it's what protects owners like me from a listing that silently goes stale.

**Rough effort:** Recurrence (model + rrule.js expansion in getEvents/events page/deconfliction, portal form controls, iCal RRULE/EXDATE output): ~1-1.5 dev-weeks. GBP write adapter behind a flag + the hasPendingEdits read-back loop + a 2-3 business pilot: ~1.5-2 dev-weeks of engineering, but gated on weeks of Chamber calendar time for GBP API-access approval and Manager cooldowns. Meta pilot (Facebook feed + IG, ≤50 testers, posting-failure alerts): ~1 dev-week. Community-poster role + moderation: ~3-5 dev-days. Total build: ~4-6 dev-weeks phased; the syndication legs are calendar-time-gated, not effort-gated. · **Rough cost:** One-time: the dev effort above; ~$0 in new licenses (rrule.js free, GBP + Meta APIs free). Recurring: effectively $0 added — stays within the existing ~$0-8/mo hosting; the only ongoing cost is human (Chamber maintaining the GBP-Manager relationships and watching posting-failure alerts). If the Chamber instead buys a social scheduler as a stopgap, budget ~$6-30/mo (approx) for Buffer/Zapier, but that's optional and I'd skip it in favor of the free GBP path.

## Ferry-timed foot-traffic capture & staffing heads-up `P0` · `Cost: Medium` · _enhances existing_

Use the live WSF feed and existing busyness forecast to (a) give owners a ~10-minute heads-up before a busy boat unloads so they can staff up, and (b) surface to just-arrived walk-ons which nearby spots are open right now and worth the walk in their 12-minute window. Turns unpredictable bursts into measurable walk-ins.

**Requested by:** Rosa Delgado, Danny Cho, Sam Okafor, Carol Whitfield, Meg Sorensen, Tom Iverson, Marcus Reed

### Approach

This is squarely an *enhancement* of infrastructure the app already owns, not a new build. The live WSF feed is wired (`src/lib/wsf.ts` → `getVesselLocations()`, `getFerryStatusSnapshot()`), the per-sailing busyness model exists (`src/lib/ferry-forecast.ts`, plus the empirical-refinement layer that learns from every logged sailing), "near me" open-now discovery already runs on `/eat`, and per-sailing ferry reminders already fire a browser `Notification` (`src/lib/ferry-reminder.ts`). The two genuinely new pieces are (1) an **owner-facing staffing heads-up** and (2) a **just-arrived "what's open right now, and can I make it back" visitor surface**. Both should reuse the ferry snapshot rather than open any new data source.

**The precise timing signal is the thing to lean on.** WSF's Vessels API returns a live `Eta` (and `EtaBasis` / `LeftDock` / `AtDock`) per vessel — that's the real "boat lands in ~N minutes" clock, far better than a static schedule. Build one small server module that joins the inbound vessel's `Eta` to that sailing's forecast level, exposes it on the existing snapshot, and drives *both* audiences from it. For the **owner heads-up**, add a per-listing opt-in in the business portal (a toggle + a configurable lead time, default 10 min, and a "only when the boat is forecast BUSY" threshold so nobody gets pinged for a near-empty evening boat). Ship it first as a **browser push notification** (same `Notification` path the rider reminder already uses) plus a dead-simple "arrivals board" the owner can leave open on a back-office tablet: the next few inbound boats, ETA countdown, forecast level, and open drive-up spaces. Add SMS only as a paid upgrade for owners who won't keep a tab open (see tools). For the **visitor surface**, extend the arrived-walk-on view: on landing (or when a rider taps their reminder), show open-now spots sorted by walk-time-from-dock, each tagged "~4 min walk · get back before your 3:45 return" using the return sailing already in the schedule feed.

**Do not ship the staffing alert on the current model.** The forecast is admin-gated OFF precisely because the backtest shows ~+22 bias — it over-predicts busy. An over-predicting model that cries "staff up!" for empty boats will burn owner trust in one bad week and they'll turn it off forever. Gate the owner heads-up behind the *same* accuracy flag, and add an owner-side calibration guardrail: only fire above a level the backtest supports, and let each owner tune their own threshold and lead time. The visitor "what's open now" surface has no such risk — it's just discovery keyed to arrival time — so **it can ship immediately, decoupled from the forecast gate**, and start delivering value (and walk-in attribution) while the model is validated against more logged sailings.

**Keep it cheap, offline-tolerant, and low-touch.** The vessel ETA already refreshes on the existing polling cadence; the arrivals board is a thin client view over the snapshot with a service-worker cache so it survives a flaky back-room signal (last-known ETAs with a "stale" chip). Attribution — "boat landed → walk-ins" — rides the privacy-first analytics already in place (coarse, opt-in, no hoarding); tie the existing near-me GPS pings and QR/menu views to the arrival timestamp to produce the "measurable walk-ins" the epic promises, without adding any new tracking.

**Build vs buy:** Build in-app, reusing what exists — do not integrate a third-party foot-traffic or staffing product. Every input (WSF Eta, forecast, open-now hours, near-me, reminders, privacy analytics) is already owned by the app, so the marginal work is a thin timing module plus two views, not a new system. The only 'buy' is an optional SMS channel (Twilio/SNS) for owners who won't keep a PWA/tab open — deep-integrate that per-message rather than routing the whole feature through it. Avoid managed push (OneSignal) unless self-hosted web-push proves hard to operate; DIY web-push keeps device tokens in-house and matches the app's privacy-first posture. Net: build the timing + visitor surface now (independent of the forecast gate), build the owner heads-up behind the accuracy flag, and treat SMS as a paid opt-in add-on.

**Rough effort:** Visitor 'what's open now / make it back' surface: ~3-5 dev-days (reuses near-me + schedule). Owner arrivals board + per-listing opt-in/threshold/lead-time in the portal: ~4-6 dev-days. Web-push plumbing (VAPID + service worker, shared with the PWA milestone): ~3-5 dev-days. Optional SMS channel incl. A2P 10DLC setup: ~2-3 dev-days. Total roughly 2-3 dev-weeks for the free-tier version; SMS and any forecast re-calibration are additive. Model validation before enabling staffing alerts is ongoing (data accrues from logged sailings). · **Rough cost:** One-time: the 2-3 dev-weeks of build above; ~$0 in new services for the free-tier (web-push) version. Recurring: ~$0 incremental on the existing ~$7.25/mo Render host (WSF API and web push are free). If SMS is added: approx $1-2/mo per number plus ~$0.008-0.01 per text — realistically single-digit to low-tens of dollars/month per opted-in owner in peak season — plus a modest one-time A2P 10DLC registration. Push-only path stays at ~$0 marginal.

## Reservations, bookings & deposit/card-hold to stop no-shows `P1` · `Cost: High` · 🔒

### Approach

## Trusted town-wide alerts & fast cancellation notices `P0` · `Cost: Medium` · 🔒 · _enhances existing_

Something owners can tell patients, guests, and staff to rely on instead of Facebook rumor.

**Requested by:** Dr. Aisha Bello, Tom Iverson, Marcus Reed, Priya Nair, Hank Boyd

### Approach

Build this as two layers on top of what we already have, not one big new system. **Layer 1 — the sourced "town status" feed — is mostly an aggregation job over official APIs we're already entitled to.** The ferry piece is done: `wsf.ts` already pulls `/schedule/alerts` and we surface service alerts + boarding-pass banners. Add SR-104 closures from the **WSDOT Highway Alerts API** (same Traveler Information API family and the *same* access-code we already registered as `WSDOT_API_KEY` — no new signup), filtered to SR-104 and high/highest impact. Add weather/storm/wind/flood advisories from the **free, keyless NWS `api.weather.gov/alerts/active` feed** scoped to our known SEW forecast zone. Each item renders with its **source name + official link + fetch timestamp** ("via WSDOT · 8:42 AM"). The hard rule the acceptance criteria demand — *never invent a status* — falls out of the architecture if we make the feed strictly a pass-through: if an upstream fetch fails or returns nothing, the card says "No ferry alerts — checked 8:42 AM" or "Status unavailable," never a fabricated "all clear." This is a new `/alerts` page plus a compact banner slot on the home live-strip, reusing the ISR + fallback-snapshot pattern the ferry status already uses. **Power outages are the honest gap:** there is no clean first-party Kitsap PUD/PSE API. **Layer 2 — owner-fired cancellations — reuses the business/nonprofit portal, not a new login.** A market manager, kayak-tour operator, or restaurant owner already has a portal account editing their listing/hours/events. **Build vs buy:** Mostly BUILD in-app by aggregating official feeds, with two deliberate deep-links-out. Realistically $0-20/mo on top of current hosting.

**LTAC funding:** Strongly LTAC-fundable: this is the tourism-promotion PLATFORM plus the exact overnight-stay and 50-mile visitor reporting RCW 67.28.1816 requires recipients to produce — arguably the most defensible spend in the whole app, since it's what documents the funding's impact. Stay clear of anything that skims a per-transaction fee to a private for-profit (e.g. paid loyalty redemptions or ordering commissions); the analytics/reporting/loyalty tooling itself is fine, but revenue channeled to individual businesses is the risky line.

**Risks:** Garbage-in on the statutory signals: the 50-mile and overnight numbers are inherently estimates from coarse geo and opt-in prompts. If we present them as precise counts a grant reviewer will (rightly) distrust them — every figure must be labeled with method + confidence, or we jeopardize the funding we're trying to defend.; Privacy overreach / clinic exposure: any drift toward per-person tracking, full IP storage, or location signals near the health-adjacent clinic listing breaks the privacy posture that makes the money legal and will burn community trust. Hard-code the clinic exclusion and the no-login-for-alerts rule.; Low-tech owner drop-off: if the dashboard reads like Google Analytics, owners won't open it and the 'they'll actually read it' acceptance criterion fails. Ruthlessly plain-English, two-minute glance, email the summary so they don't even have to log in.; Loyalty cost/scale creep: per-business SaaS loyalty (Loopy/Stamp Me) doesn't scale to 166 members and risks pushing us toward commission models that are LTAC-off-limits. If the DIY Wallet-pass build slips, the fallback must stay opt-in and non-commissioned, not chamber-wide.
